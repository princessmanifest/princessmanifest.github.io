Ghosts in the Mind

Mapping Death and Delusion in America

What drives people to report haunted places?
This visualization explores potential links between rates of Alzheimer's Disease, Drug Overdose, and Alcohol Abuse and the number of reported haunted locations across the United States. By selecting each cause of death, you can observe patterns that may hint at how cognitive decline, substance use, and emotional instability could influence perceptions of paranormal activity.

Alzheimer's Disease: Alzheimer's Disease is associated with memory loss, confusion, and altered perceptions of reality. When viewing Alzheimer's rates, states like Mississippi, Washington, Vermont, Arkansas, and Texas show darker shades, indicating higher death rates. These regions may experience heightened vulnerabilities to cognitive distortion, potentially contributing to a greater number of haunted place reports.
